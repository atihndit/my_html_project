    • Create a GitHub account and sign in.
    • Create a new repository by clicking on the "+" sign in the top right corner of your GitHub dashboard and select "New repository".
    • Give your repository a name, such as "my-html-project".
    • Check the box to "Initialize this repository with a README"
    • Click the "Create repository" button.
    • Now that you have created your repository, you can upload your HTML files to it:
    • Click the "Add file" button, then select "Upload files".
    • Drag and drop your HTML files or click on "choose your files" to select them from your computer.
    • Once your files have finished uploading, click on the "Commit changes" button to save them to your repository.
    • Finally, to make your HTML project available online, you need to enable GitHub Pages:
    • Go to your repository's settings by clicking on the "Settings" tab.
    • Scroll down to the "GitHub Pages" section and select "Master branch" as the source.
    • Click on the "Save" button and wait a few minutes for GitHub to build and publish your website !
